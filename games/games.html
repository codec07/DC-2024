Archery

Archery is an ancient skill that dates back to prehistoric times when it was primarily used for hunting and warfare. Over the centuries, it evolved into a competitive sport, gaining prominence in the 19th century.

Badminton

Badminton is a fast-paced racquet sport played on a rectangular court, where players aim to hit a shuttlecock over a net. It became an Olympic sport in 1992 and requires quick reflexes, agility, and precision. Played in singles or doubles, badminton is popular worldwide.

Basketball

Basketball is a dynamic team sport where two teams of five players compete to score by shooting a ball through the opponent's hoop. It became an Olympic sport in 1936 and is celebrated for its fast pace, athleticism, and strategic plays

Boxing

Boxing is a combat sport where two athletes throw punches at each other in a controlled environment. It has been part of the Olympics since 1904 and demands a combination of physical strength, endurance, and technique. Boxing is divided into weight classes to ensure fair competition.

Breaking

Breaking, also known as breakdancing, is a form of street dance that will debut as an Olympic sport in 2024. It combines athletic dance moves, including spins, flips, and complex footwork. Breaking emphasizes creativity, rhythm, and physical strength, making it a unique addition to the Olympics.

Canoe

Canoeing in the Olympics includes both sprint and slalom events, where athletes navigate a narrow boat using a single-bladed paddle. The sport, which debuted in the 1936 Olympics, tests athletes' strength, balance, and paddling skills over various distances and challenging water courses.

Cycling

Olympic cycling includes various disciplines such as road, track, mountain biking, and BMX. Each discipline tests different aspects of cycling ability, from endurance to speed. Cycling has been part of the Olympics since 1896 and continues to be one of the most physically demanding sports.

Equestrian

Equestrian events in the Olympics include dressage, eventing, and jumping, where riders compete with their horses. It is unique in that it is the only Olympic sport where humans and animals work as a team. Equestrian events have been part of the Olympics since 1900.

Fencing

Fencing is a traditional sport involving combat with swords, including foil, epee, and sabre. It has been part of the modern Olympic Games since their inception in 1896. The sport is based on precision, strategy, and fast reflexes as athletes attempt to score points by landing touches on their opponent.

Football

Football, or soccer, is one of the most popular team sports worldwide, played in the Olympics since 1900 for men and 1996 for women. Teams of 11 players compete to score goals by advancing a ball into the opponent's net. It is known for its global following and strategic team play.

Golf

Golf returned to the Olympics in 2016 after a 112-year absence. It involves players competing to complete a course in the fewest number of strokes. Precision, control, and strategy are key components of this sport, which is played individually over 18 holes.

Swimming

Swimming is a staple Olympic sport that includes various styles such as freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, and butterfly. It tests athletes' speed, endurance, and technique in individual and relay races. Swimming has been part of the Olympics since the first modern Games in 1896.
